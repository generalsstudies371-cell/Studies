Chota Nagpur Tenancy (CNT) Act, 1908

Essential Notes for JPSC Paper II (Jharkhand GS)

1. Important Legal Definitions (Section 3)

Term Legal Explanation
Mundari Khunt-Kattidari The interest of a Mundari Khunt-Kattidar.
Permanent Tenure A heritable tenure not held for a limited time.
Proprietor A person owning an estate (in trust or for own benefit).
Rent Lawful payment (money/kind) by a tenant to a landlord; excludes personal service.
Tenant A person who holds land under another and is liable to pay rent.
Tenure Interest of a tenure-holder; excludes Mundari Khunt-Kattidari tenancy.
Village Headman Known as Manki, Pradhan, Manjhi, Thikadar, or Ijardar.
JPSC Tip: Remember that "Tenure" explicitly excludes Mundari Khunt-Kattidari. This is a frequent MCQ topic!

2. Classes of Tenants (Section 4)

Hierarchy: Proprietor → Tenure-holder → Raiyat → Under-Raiyat
  • 1. Tenure-holder: Includes under-tenure holders.
  • 2. Raiyat (Cultivating Tenants):
    • Occupancy Raiyats: Rights gained usually after 12 years.
    • Non-occupancy Raiyats: Temporary tenants.
    • Raiyats with Khunt-Katti rights: Special ancestral rights.
  • 3. Under-Raiyat: Holds land indirectly under a Raiyat.
  • 4. Mundari Khunt-Kattidar: Male descendants of the original jungle clearers.

ЁЯУЬ Chotanagpur Tenancy Act, 1908 (CNT Act)

ЁЯФ╣ Overview

The Chotanagpur Tenancy Act, 1908 was enacted to protect the interest of aboriginals residing within the Chotanagpur region.

  • Total Chapters: 19
  • Total Sections: 271
  • Total Schedules: 2

ЁЯФ╣ Area Covered

  • Palamu
  • Kolhan
  • North Chotanagpur
  • South Chotanagpur

ЁЯФ╣ Chapter I (Section 1 to 4)

  • Identity of Act
  • Area of operation
  • Repeal of earlier tenancy laws
  • Definitions

ЁЯФ╣ Section 1 тАУ Name & Extent

  • Name: Chotanagpur Tenancy Act, 1908
  • Extent: North Chotanagpur, South Chotanagpur & Palamu divisions
  • Includes: Municipal areas, Notified areas & Cantonment areas

ЁЯФ╣ Section 2 тАУ Repeal

  • Schedule A: General repeal in Chotanagpur
  • Schedule B: Area-specific repeal (Dhanbad & certain police stations)

ЁЯФ╣ Section 3 тАУ Definitions

  • Holding: Parcel of land held by a raiyat forming a separate tenancy
  • Korkar: Land artificially levelled or embanked primarily for cultivation of rice
  • Landlord: Person immediately under whom tenant holds land (includes Government)
  • Mundari Khunt-Kattidar Tenancy: Interest of a Mundari Khunt-Kattidar
  • Permanent Tenure: Heritable tenure not held for a limited time
  • Proprietor: Person owning land as estate or part of estate
  • Rent: Lawfully payable money or kind by tenant (excluding personal service)
  • Tenant: Person holding land under another and liable to pay rent
  • Tenure: Interest of a tenure-holder (does not include Mundari Khunt-Katti)
  • Village Headman: Manki, Manjhi, Pradhan, Tikkadar or Ijardaar

ЁЯФ╣ Section 4 тАУ Classes of Tenants

  • 1. Tenure-holders (including under-tenure holders)
  • 2. Raiyats (Cultivating tenants)
    • Occupancy Raiyats тАУ Right after 12 years of continuous holding
    • Non-Occupancy Raiyats тАУ Temporary tenants
    • Raiyats having Khunt-Katti rights
  • 3. Under-Raiyats тАУ Tenants under a raiyat
  • 4. Mundari Khunt-Kattidars тАУ Mundari who cleared jungle land and their male descendants

Important Definitions

Term Legal Meaning
Mundari Khunt-Kattidari Interest of a Mundari Khunt-Kattidar.
Permanent Tenure Heritable tenure, not held for a limited time.
Proprietor Person owning an estate (in trust or for own benefit).
Rent Lawfully payable in money or kind to a landlord for land use.
Village Headman Known as Manki, Pradhan, Manjhi, Thikadar, or Ijardar.

Section 4: Classes of Tenants

1. Tenure Holder
Includes under tenure-holders.
2. Raiyat (Cultivating Tenants)
• Occupancy Raiyats: Rights acquired usually after 12 years.
• Non-occupancy: Temporary tenants without rights.
• Khunt-Katti Rights: Raiyats with specific ancestral rights.
3. Under-Raiyat
Holds land under a Raiyat; indirect cultivation.
4. Mundari Khunt-Kattidar
Mundari who cleared jungle for land; rights pass through the male line.

ğŸ“˜ The Chotanagpur Tenancy Act, 1908 (CNT Act)

ğŸ“ Area Covered

  • Palamu
  • Kolhan
  • North Chotanagpur (Hazaribagh)
  • South Chotanagpur (Ranchi)

ğŸ¯ Objective of the Act

The Chotanagpur Tenancy Act, 1908 was enacted to protect the interests of aboriginals (tribals) residing within the Chotanagpur region.

ğŸ“š Structure of the Act

  • Total Chapters: 19
  • Total Sections: 271
  • Total Schedules: 2

ğŸ“– Chapter I (Sections 1 to 4)

  • Identity of the Act
  • Area of operation
  • Repeal of earlier tenancy laws
  • Definitions

ğŸ”¹ Section 1: Short Title & Extent

Name: Chotanagpur Tenancy Act, 1908

Extent: North Chotanagpur, South Chotanagpur and Palamu divisions

Includes: Municipal areas, Notified Area Committees and Cantonment areas

ğŸ”¹ Section 2: Repeal of Earlier Tenancy Laws

  • Schedule A: General repeal in Chotanagpur region
  • Schedule B: Area-specific repeal (Dhanbad and certain police station areas)

ğŸ”¹ Section 3: Definitions

Holding: A parcel or parcels of land held by a raiyat, forming the subject of a separate tenancy.

Korkar: Land artificially levelled or embanked primarily for the cultivation of rice.

Landlord: A person immediately under whom a tenant holds land and includes the Government.

Mundari Khunt-Kattidar Tenancy: Means the interest of a Mundari Khunt-Kattidar.

Permanent Tenure: A tenure which is heritable and not held for a limited time.

Proprietor: A person owning land whether in trust or for his own benefit, as an estate or part of an estate.

Rent: Whatever is lawfully payable in money or kind by a tenant to his landlord on account of use or occupation of land, excluding personal service.

Tenant: A person who holds land under another and is liable to pay rent for that land.

Tenure: The interest of a tenure-holder including under-tenure, but does not include Mundari Khunt-Kattidari tenancy.

Village Headman: Headman of a village or group of villages, known as Manki, Manjhi, Pradhan or by any equivalent designation such as Tikkadar or Ijardaar.

ğŸ”¹ Section 4: Classes of Tenants

  1. Tenure-holders (including under-tenure holders)
  2. Raiyats (Cultivating tenants)
    • Occupancy Raiyats: Have right of occupancy, usually after 12 years of continuous holding.
    • Non-Occupancy Raiyats: Do not have occupancy rights; temporary tenants.
    • Raiyats having Khunt-Katti rights
  3. Under-Raiyats: Tenants under a raiyat who cultivate land but hold it indirectly.
  4. Mundari Khunt-Kattidars: Mundaris who cleared jungle land, made it fit for cultivation, and include their descendants in the male line.

  • Khunt-Katti system is very important for JPSC

Section 3 Definitions
Holding:
A parcel or parcels of land held by a raiyat and forming the subject of a separate tenancy.

Korkar:
Land artificially levelled or embanked primarily for the cultivation of rice.

Landlord:
A person immediately under whom a tenant holds land and includes the Government.

Mundari Khunt-Kattidar Tenancy:
Means the interest of a Mundari Khunt-Kattidar.

Permanent Tenure:
A tenure which is heritable and not held for a limited period.

Proprietor:
A person owning land, whether in trust or for own benefit, as an estate or part of an estate.

Rent:
Whatever is lawfully payable in money or kind by a tenant to his landlord for use or occupation of land, excluding personal service.

Tenant:
A person who holds land under another and is or would be liable to pay rent for that land.

Tenure:
The interest of a tenure-holder and includes an under-tenure, but does not include Mundari Khunt-Kattidari tenancy.

Village Headman:
Headman of a village or group of villages, known as Manki, Manjhi, Pradhan or any equivalent designation such as Tikkadar or Ijardaar.

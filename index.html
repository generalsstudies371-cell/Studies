Complete Notes: CNT Act 1908 & SPT Act 1949

CNT Act: Chapter 1 & 2

Important Definitions

TermDefinition from Notes
Mundari Khunt-Kattidari tenancymeans the interest of a Mundari Khunt-Kattidar.
Permanent tenuremeans a tenure which is heritable and which is not held for a limited time.
Proprietormeans a person owning, whether in trust or for his own benefit, an estate or part of estate.
Rentmeans whatever is lawfully payable in money or kind by a tenant to his landlord on account of use or occupation of the land held by the tenant, and includes all dues (other than personal service) which are recoverable under any law for the time being in force as if they were rent.
Tenantmeans a person who holds land under another person and is, or but for a special contract would be, liable to pay rent for that land to that person.
Tenuremeans the interest of a tenure-holder, and includes an under-tenure but does not include a Mundari Khunt-Kattidari tenancy.
Village Headmanmeans the headman of a village or of a group of villages, whether known as manki, pradhan, manjhi or otherwise, or by any equivalent designation, such as Thikadar or Ijardar.

SPT Act, 1949

Structure: The Act is divided into 11 Chapters.
Key Restrictions: • General prohibition on the transfer of holdings (Chapter V). • Rent is strictly restricted to cash only (Chapter VII).
Legal Power: • Jurisdiction of Civil Courts is barred (Chapter IX). • Deputy Commissioner holds original jurisdiction for ejectment and waste land settlement.
